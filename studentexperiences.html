Ryan Jennings

At Oklahoma Christian, I have consistently been challenged in my academics. There is always something new to learn. The vast amount of content available can be overwhelming, but the teachers are always readily available to help you. On top of that, there are so many online resources that can help with classes, it is very realistic to consistently succeed in the Computer Science program. Upperclassmen are extremely kind and helpful, and are always willing to give advice on how to work smarter and be successful.

Caleb Bozarth

Initially when I came here, I wanted to be an Electrical Engineer because my step-dad is an electritian, so I thought I would enjoy it. To get a major in Electrical Engineering, students have to take several programming courses. I did my first semester and I was taking a programming course and several Electrical Engineering classes. I had come to the conclusion that I did not want to continue in Electrical Engineering. I looked at what classes I was taking and found out the class I enjoyed the most was Programming I, so I talked to my professor and he advised me and helped me make the decision to switch. I decided to switch to Computer Science, and I truly believe that it was the best decision I could have made. I love my professors ( seriously, every one of them ), and I love the classes. The course material is very appropriately challenging and it is perfect for teaching all of the things that one needs to know to be a part of the Computer Science industry. Getting a degree in Computer Science at Oklahoma Christian University is the greatest decision I ever made and could be yours too!
